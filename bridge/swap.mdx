The RustChain-Solana bridge converts your native RTC to wRTC (Wrapped RTC on Solana) so you can access Solana DeFi, DEX trading, and cross-chain payments. This guide walks you through the full swap process, from pre-flight checks to confirming your wRTC on the Solana side.

<Warning>
  This bridge is early-stage. Liquidity is thin. Only swap amounts you can afford to
  lose value on in a thin market. Large orders will move the price significantly —
  check pool depth before proceeding.
</Warning>

## Before You Swap

Work through this checklist before initiating a bridge request:

- [ ] **You have a native RTC balance.** Check your balance: `curl -fsS "https://rustchain.org/wallet/balance?miner_id=YOUR_WALLET_NAME"`
- [ ] **You have a Solana wallet.** Phantom, Backpack, and Solflare all work. Have your Solana wallet address ready (base58, ≥32 characters).
- [ ] **You understand the liquidity risk.** The wRTC pool is thin. Your swap may settle at a worse price than you expect. Do not swap more than you are comfortable with.
- [ ] **Minimum swap: 1 RTC. Maximum swap: 10,000 RTC per transaction.**
- [ ] **Your RTC address starts with `RTC`.** The bridge requires a verified RustChain `RTC...` wallet address, not a miner ID.

## Bridge Instructions

### Step 1 — Confirm your RTC balance

```bash
curl -fsS "https://rustchain.org/wallet/balance?miner_id=YOUR_WALLET_NAME"
```

Note the `balance` field in the response. Make sure you have enough RTC for the amount you want to bridge, plus the 0.0001 RTC transfer fee.

### Step 2 — Initiate the bridge lock

Submit a lock request to the bridge API with your RTC wallet address, the amount, your target Solana wallet, and the RustChain transaction hash of the funds you are locking:

```json
POST /bridge/lock

{
  "sender_wallet":  "my-rtc-wallet",
  "amount":         100.0,
  "target_chain":   "solana",
  "target_wallet":  "7xKXtg2CW87d97TXJSDpbD5jBkheTqA83TZRuJosgAsU",
  "tx_hash":        "your-rustchain-lock-tx-hash"
}
```

The bridge returns a `lock_id` and sets the state to `requested`. Keep the `lock_id` — you'll use it to track the status of your swap.

### Step 3 — Wait for proof confirmation

In Phase 1, a bridge admin reviews your transaction proof before minting wRTC. Check your lock status using the `lock_id`:

```bash
curl -fsS "https://rustchain.org/bridge/status/YOUR_LOCK_ID"
```

The state moves from `requested` → `confirmed` → `complete` as the bridge processes your swap.

### Step 4 — Confirm wRTC in your Solana wallet

Once the state reaches `complete`, open your Solana wallet and verify the wRTC SPL token balance. You can also verify on-chain using the [DexScreener chart](https://dexscreener.com/solana/8CF2Q8nSCxRacDShbtF86XTSrYjueBMKmfdR3MLdnYzb) or the [Raydium swap interface](https://raydium.io/swap/?inputMint=sol&outputMint=12TAdKXxcGf6oCv4rqDz2NkgxjyHq6HQKoxKZYGf5i4X).

## Converting wRTC Back to RTC

To reverse the bridge and recover native RTC:

1. **Burn your wRTC** on the Solana side using the bridge interface at [bottube.ai/bridge/wrtc](https://bottube.ai/bridge/wrtc).
2. **Submit the burn transaction hash** to the bridge release endpoint.
3. **Wait for confirmation.** The bridge verifies the burn on Solana and releases the locked RTC back to your RustChain wallet.
4. **Confirm your RTC balance** with the balance endpoint above.

The reverse process follows the same confirmation flow as the forward bridge. Allow time for both chains to confirm before contacting support.

## Checking wRTC Holders

The RustChain repository maintains a transparent record of wRTC holder data in the `wrtc_holders/` directory. You can inspect this directory in the [Rustchain GitHub repo](https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain/tree/main/wrtc_holders) to see historical holder snapshots and supply data.

## Bridge Lock States

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| `requested` | Lock received; awaiting admin proof confirmation |
| `confirmed` | Proof confirmed; bridge is preparing to mint wRTC |
| `releasing` | Admin is minting wRTC on Solana |
| `complete` | wRTC minted and delivered to your Solana wallet |
| `failed` | Lock failed; contact support with your `lock_id` |
| `refunded` | RTC returned to your RustChain wallet |
