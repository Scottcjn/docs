RustChain reward multipliers are based on hardware rarity and preservation value, not processing power. A machine that costs $35 and can be ordered by the thousand earns almost nothing. A machine from 1979 that still boots and computes — and that took effort to keep alive — earns the most. The multiplier system encodes this philosophy in every epoch reward.

<Warning>
The ARM penalty exists to prevent cheap SBC farms from flooding the network. A Raspberry Pi costs $35 — someone could spin up thousands. The 0.0005x rate makes farming economically pointless while still allowing legitimate bare-metal ARM owners to participate. **Vintage ARM** (ARM2, ARM3, ARM7TDMI, StrongARM, XScale, Cortex-A8/A9) is a completely different category and receives full antiquity multipliers — see the Legendary/Mythic table above.
</Warning>

## How Multipliers Are Calculated

Your epoch reward is not just the base multiplier — two dynamic adjustments apply depending on whether your hardware is vintage or modern.

### Time Decay (Vintage Hardware)

Vintage hardware (more than five years old) applies a decay factor to its vintage bonus. This rewards early RustChain adopters who started mining before time decay eroded the bonus.

```
decay_factor     = 1.0 − (0.15 × (hardware_age − 5) / 5.0)
vintage_bonus    = base_multiplier − 1.0
final_multiplier = 1.0 + (vintage_bonus × decay_factor)
```

**Example — PowerPC G4 (base 2.5x, age 24 years):**
```
decay_factor  = 1.0 − (0.15 × 19 / 5.0) = 0.43
vintage_bonus = 2.5 − 1.0 = 1.5
final         = 1.0 + (1.5 × 0.43) = 1.645x
```

**Example — Core 2 Duo E8400 (base 1.3x, age ~19 years):**
```
decay_factor  = 1.0 − (0.15 × 14 / 5.0) = 0.58
vintage_bonus = 1.3 − 1.0 = 0.3
final         = 1.0 + (0.3 × 0.58) = 1.174x
```

### Loyalty Bonus (Modern Hardware)

Modern CPUs (five years old or newer) accumulate a loyalty bonus of **+15% per year of continuous uptime**, capped at +50%.

```
loyalty_bonus    = min(0.5, uptime_years × 0.15)
final_multiplier = base_multiplier + loyalty_bonus
```

| Years Uptime | Bonus | Final (1.0x base) |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | +0% | 1.0x |
| 1 | +15% | 1.15x |
| 2 | +30% | 1.3x |
| 3 | +45% | 1.45x |
| 4+ | +50% (cap) | 1.5x |

### Server Bonus

Enterprise-class CPUs (Intel Xeon, AMD EPYC, AMD Opteron) receive an additional **+10%** on top of their calculated multiplier.

**Example — Xeon E5-1650 v2 (Ivy Bridge, age ~13 years):**
```
base          = 1.1x (Ivy Bridge)
with_decay    = 1.0 + (0.1 × decay_factor) ≈ 1.076x
with_server   = 1.076 × 1.1 = 1.184x
```

## Improving Your Multiplier

Use these strategies to maximize your epoch earnings:

**Run rare architectures.** A PowerPC G4, a Sun SPARC workstation, or any MYTHIC/LEGENDARY machine earns dramatically more than modern x86 from day one. Check the [preserved machines tracker](https://rustchain.org/preserved.html) for examples of what other miners are running.

**Keep your machine running continuously.** Both the vintage decay system and the modern loyalty bonus reward consistent uptime. Gaps in attestation history can reset your loyalty streak.

**Start mining early.** Time decay on vintage hardware is real — a G4 that started mining in 2025 earns more per epoch than the same G4 starting in 2030. The best time to plug it in is now.

**Use bare metal.** The hardware fingerprinting system rejects VMs. A machine that fails attestation earns nothing regardless of its theoretical multiplier.

<Info>
Multipliers are assigned automatically by the hardware attestation system based on your CPU's detected architecture and age. You do not submit or claim a multiplier manually — the server derives it from the six-check fingerprint and cross-validates against known architecture signatures.
</Info>
