Proof-of-Antiquity (PoA) is the consensus mechanism that powers RustChain. Instead of rewarding the fastest hardware or the largest stake, PoA rewards the *oldest* hardware — and the longer you keep that hardware running, the more RTC you earn. If you're mining on a PowerPC G4, a vintage 486, or a DEC VAX, you're not running outdated equipment: you're running some of the highest-earning nodes on the entire network.

## How It Works

RustChain divides time into **epochs**. Each epoch spans 144 blocks at a 10-minute block time, making each epoch roughly 24 hours long. At the close of every epoch, the network distributes a reward pool of RTC to every miner that successfully attested during that period.

Here's what happens inside each epoch:

1. **Hardware attestation** — Your miner submits a hardware fingerprint to the attestation nodes. This fingerprint proves you're running real physical silicon, not a VM or emulator. Six independent checks run on every submission (see [Hardware Fingerprinting](/concepts/hardware-fingerprinting)).
2. **Multiplier assignment** — Once your hardware passes attestation, the server assigns your antiquity multiplier based on your CPU's architecture, age, and rarity.
3. **Reward distribution** — At epoch close, the pool (1.5 RTC during the genesis period) is split among all attesting miners, weighted by their multipliers. A machine with a 2.5x multiplier earns more than twice what a 1.0x modern machine earns from the same pool.

The rule is **1 CPU = 1 vote**. Unlike Proof-of-Work, running more CPU threads or buying a faster chip earns you nothing extra. What matters is *what* you're running, not how hard it runs.

## Why Age Matters

Every other blockchain treats hardware as a liability the moment something newer ships. Proof-of-Work forces you to upgrade to the latest ASICs or fall behind. Proof-of-Stake ignores hardware entirely and rewards the largest wallet.

RustChain inverts that logic. Your hardware **appreciates** as it ages, for two reasons:

- **Rarity increases over time.** A working PowerPC G4 is harder to find today than it was in 2003. Running one signals genuine preservation effort.
- **Manufacturing emissions are already spent.** Keeping an old machine running avoids the CO₂ cost of manufacturing a new one. RustChain's fleet of 16+ preserved machines draws roughly the same power as a single modern GPU rig while preventing an estimated 1,300 kg of manufacturing CO₂ and 250 kg of e-waste.

The philosophical bet at RustChain's core is this: if your machine still computes, it has value. The network is designed to keep it alive.

```
2026:  Your Ryzen 9 mines at 1.0x         ░░░░░░░░░░
2031:  Same machine, now "retro" at 1.3x  ░░░░░░░░░░░░░
2036:  Vintage tier unlocked at 1.8x      ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░
2041:  Ancient tier — 2.2x and climbing   ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░
       ↑ Same hardware. Same owner. Growing rewards.
```

## Multiplier Tiers

RustChain groups hardware into tiers by rarity and preservation value. Your base multiplier comes from whichever tier your CPU falls into.

| Tier | Multiplier Range | Example Hardware |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **MYTHIC** | 3.5x – 4.0x | Acorn ARM2 (4.0x), DEC VAX-11/780 (3.5x), Inmos Transputer (3.5x) |
| **LEGENDARY** | 2.5x – 3.5x | Motorola 68000 (3.0x), Sun SPARC v7 (2.9x), SGI MIPS R4000 (2.8x) |
| **ANCIENT** | 2.0x – 2.5x | PowerPC G4 (2.5x), PS3 Cell BE (2.2x), PowerPC G5 (2.0x) |
| **MODERN** | 1.0x | Zen 3+, Skylake+, Coffee Lake, Alder Lake |
| **ARM PENALTY** | 0.0005x | aarch64 NAS/SBC, Raspberry Pi 4/5, AWS Graviton |

<Info>
Multipliers are determined automatically during hardware attestation. You do not need to claim or configure a tier — the system detects your CPU and assigns the correct value.
</Info>

## Time Decay and Loyalty Bonuses

Base multipliers are only the starting point. RustChain applies two dynamic adjustments that reward commitment on both ends of the age spectrum.

### Time Decay for Vintage Hardware

Vintage machines (more than five years old) have their bonus portion reduced by **15% per year** of chain operation. This rewards early adopters who started mining when the chain launched.

The formula:

```
decay_factor    = 1.0 − (0.15 × (hardware_age − 5) / 5.0)
final_multiplier = 1.0 + (vintage_bonus × decay_factor)
```

Even with decay applied, vintage hardware still outearns modern hardware. A PowerPC G4 with a base of 2.5x, after 24 years of hardware age, resolves to approximately **1.645x** — still well above the 1.0x modern baseline.

### Loyalty Bonus for Modern Hardware

Modern CPUs (five years old or newer) start at their base multiplier and earn **+15% per year of continuous uptime**, capped at +50%.

| Years of Uptime | Bonus | Final Multiplier (1.0x base) |
|-----------------|-------|------------------------------|
| 0 | +0% | 1.0x |
| 1 | +15% | 1.15x |
| 2 | +30% | 1.3x |
| 3 | +45% | 1.45x |
| 4+ | +50% (cap) | 1.5x |

Start mining today on modern hardware and the patience pays off. In four years of consistent uptime, a Ryzen 9 or a Raptor Lake Core i9 reaches **1.5x** — competitive with some vintage machines.

## How Proof-of-Antiquity Compares to PoW and PoS

| | Proof-of-Work | Proof-of-Stake | Proof-of-Antiquity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What earns rewards** | Raw hash rate | Token holdings | Hardware age + rarity |
| **Hardware advantage** | Newest ASICs win | Hardware irrelevant | Oldest hardware wins |
| **Energy use** | Very high | Very low | Low (vintage hardware) |
| **Anti-spam** | Hash difficulty | Stake requirement | AI fingerprinting |
| **Long-term dynamic** | Hardware depreciates | Wealth compounds | Hardware appreciates |

Where Proof-of-Work turns old hardware into scrap and Proof-of-Stake rewards the already wealthy, Proof-of-Antiquity turns the passage of time into a mining advantage. Every machine on the network gets older every day — and with age comes higher rewards.

## VM and Emulator Submissions

Attempting to mine from a virtual machine, Docker container, cloud instance, or software emulator (including SheepShaver or QEMU pretending to be vintage hardware) is detected by the hardware fingerprinting system.

<Warning>
VMs and emulators are flagged at **0x** — they receive no rewards. Attempting to spoof hardware fingerprints results in permanent blacklisting of the wallet and machine identifier. Mine on bare metal only.
</Warning>
