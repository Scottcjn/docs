Find answers to the most common questions about RustChain mining, the RTC token, and hardware compatibility.

## Mining

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Do I need special hardware?">
    No. Any computer that runs Python 3.8 or later can mine RTC — Linux, macOS, Windows, PowerPC Macs, SPARC workstations, Raspberry Pis, and more are all supported.

    That said, **older hardware earns more.** RustChain's Proof-of-Antiquity consensus assigns multipliers based on how rare and vintage your hardware is. A modern x86\_64 desktop mines at a 0.8x baseline, while a PowerBook G4 mines at 2.5x and a Motorola 68000 machine mines at 3.0x.

    Install the miner on whatever machine you have today — all hardware eventually becomes vintage, and your multiplier grows with age.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I mine on a Raspberry Pi?">
    Yes, but expect a **0.0005x multiplier** — a steep penalty by design.

    ARM single-board computers like the Raspberry Pi are inexpensive and easy to acquire in bulk, which makes them a prime vector for farming attacks. The 0.0005x penalty makes it economically unviable to run large ARM farms while still allowing genuine Pi owners to participate.

    If you have a Pi, connect it — but do not expect significant rewards compared to vintage x86 or PowerPC hardware.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I mine in a VM or container?">
    No. RustChain's hardware fingerprinting actively detects and penalizes virtual environments.

    The attestation system runs six checks per machine — oscillator drift, cache timing, SIMD identity, thermal entropy, instruction jitter, and anti-emulation detection. VMs (VMware, VirtualBox, QEMU, Hyper-V, WSL), Docker containers, LXC, and Kubernetes environments all trigger these checks and receive approximately **1 billionth** of normal rewards.

    Run the miner on bare metal only.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long until I get my first reward?">
    Wait at least **one full epoch (~24 hours)** after enrollment before expecting rewards.

    Epochs are \~10-minute windows during which 1.5 RTC is distributed to all attesting miners. However, a newly enrolled miner typically needs to complete at least one full daily epoch cycle to appear in the reward distribution. Do not treat a zero balance in the first 20–30 minutes as a failure — check again after a few epochs have settled.

    Confirm your miner is active on the network:

    ```bash
    curl -sk https://rustchain.org/api/miners
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I run multiple miners?">
    Yes. Run one miner per physical machine, each with a **unique wallet name** on different hardware.

    Each unique hardware device earns one consensus participation slot per epoch. Running two instances on the same machine does not increase your rewards — it just causes conflicts. Spread across multiple physical machines (each with its own `--wallet` name) and you earn one slot's worth of rewards per machine.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Rewards & token

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="What is RTC worth?">
    The internal reference rate used for bounty accounting is approximately **$0.10–$0.15 USD per RTC**, depending on the current holder milestone.

    <Callout type="warning">
      This is an **internal reference rate**, not a market price or a guarantee of convertibility. The wRTC pool on Solana is early-stage with thin liquidity — treat it as experimental. Always check the live rate at [`/api/tokenomics`](https://rustchain.org/api/tokenomics) and the [DexScreener chart](https://dexscreener.com/solana/8CF2Q8nSCxRacDShbtF86XTSrYjueBMKmfdR3MLdnYzb) for current market conditions.
    </Callout>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the total RTC supply?">
    **8,388,608 RTC** — exactly 2²³. This cap is fixed forever and enforced by consensus.

    Supply distribution:

    | Allocation | Share | RTC |
    |------------|-------|-----|
    | Block mining rewards | 94% | 7,885,292 |
    | Founders | 1.5% | 125,829 |
    | Dev fund | 1.5% | 125,829 |
    | Team / bounties | 1.5% | 125,829 |
    | Community | 1.5% | 125,829 |

    Premine wallets have a one-year on-chain unlock delay. There was no VC pre-sale and no private allocation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I withdraw or trade RTC?">
    Use the **wRTC Solana bridge** to convert native RTC into wrapped RTC (wRTC) on Solana, where you can trade it on DEXs like Raydium.

    | Resource | Link |
    |----------|------|
    | Bridge | [bottube.ai/bridge/wrtc](https://bottube.ai/bridge/wrtc) |
    | Swap (Raydium) | [Raydium DEX](https://raydium.io/swap/?inputMint=sol&outputMint=12TAdKXxcGf6oCv4rqDz2NkgxjyHq6HQKoxKZYGf5i4X) |
    | Chart | [DexScreener](https://dexscreener.com/solana/8CF2Q8nSCxRacDShbtF86XTSrYjueBMKmfdR3MLdnYzb) |
    | Guide | [wRTC Overview](/bridge/wrtc-overview) |

    The bridge is live, but the liquidity pool is early-stage. Treat large trades with caution.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why did my multiplier change?">
    Two forces move your multiplier over time:

    - **Time decay on vintage hardware:** Exceptionally old or rare machines may see small adjustments as the scoring model evolves to reflect current preservation value.
    - **Loyalty bonus on modern hardware:** Modern hardware (e.g., a current-generation x86 or Apple Silicon machine) starts at its base multiplier and accumulates a loyalty bonus the longer it stays enrolled and active. Over years, today's modern hardware crosses into "retro," then "vintage," then "ancient" tiers — and the multiplier climbs accordingly.

    Your hardware's value in RustChain only increases with time. The longer you keep a machine running, the more it earns.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Why does my MacBook M1 get only 1.2x?">
    Because it is modern hardware — and that is not a problem.

    The M1 shipped in 2020. It is fast, efficient, and welcome on the network. But RustChain's multipliers reward **preservation and rarity**, not raw performance. The M1 is neither rare nor vintage yet.

    Here is the good news: **wait.** In 5 years, the M1 crosses into retro territory. In 10 years, vintage. In 20 years, it is a museum piece with a multiplier to match. Start mining now and your multiplier grows automatically — no action required. The same machine, the same owner, climbing rewards over time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the highest multiplier possible?">
    **4.0x** — awarded to the **Acorn ARM2 (1987)**, the CPU where ARM began.

    Other top-tier machines:

    | Hardware | Multiplier |
    |----------|-----------|
    | Acorn ARM2 (1987) | 4.0x |
    | DEC VAX-11/780 (1977) | 3.5x |
    | Inmos Transputer (1984) | 3.5x |
    | Motorola 68000 (1979) | 3.0x |
    | Sun SPARC (1987) | 2.9x |

    If you have one of these machines in working condition, plug it in. It is the rarest iron on the network.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will my hardware's multiplier increase over time?">
    Yes — for all hardware, guaranteed.

    Modern hardware accumulates a **loyalty bonus** while it is enrolled and actively mining. That bonus compounds as the hardware ages through RustChain's tiers: Modern → Uncommon → Rare → Ancient → Legendary → Mythic.

    You do not need to do anything. Keep the machine running, keep the miner active, and let time do the work. RustChain is the only network where your hardware **appreciates** instead of depreciating.
  </Accordion>

## Testnet & development

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Is there a testnet?">
    Yes. RustChain has a testnet environment with a faucet that dispenses **0.5 test RTC per 24 hours per IP address**.

    Use the testnet to experiment with wallet operations, test integrations, and try the API without spending real RTC.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get testnet RTC?">
    Send a `POST` request to the `/faucet/drip` endpoint with your wallet address:

    ```bash
    curl -s -X POST https://rustchain.org/faucet/drip \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d '{"wallet": "YOUR_WALLET_ADDRESS"}'
    ```

    A successful response looks like:

    ```json
    {
      "ok": true,
      "amount": 0.5,
      "wallet": "YOUR_WALLET_ADDRESS",
      "next_available": "2026-03-09T14:20:00"
    }
    ```

    The faucet enforces a rate limit of 0.5 RTC per 24 hours per IP. If you hit the limit, the response includes a `next_available` timestamp telling you when you can request again.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## General

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Is RustChain open source?">
    Yes. RustChain is licensed under the **Apache 2.0 license**.

    Browse the source, open issues, and submit pull requests at [github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain](https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain).

    Contributions earn RTC bounties. See the [bounty board](https://github.com/Scottcjn/rustchain-bounties/issues) for open tasks ranging from 1 RTC (documentation fixes) to 150 RTC (critical vulnerability discoveries).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there VC funding?">
    **$0 in VC funding.** RustChain was built on pawn shop hardware with no private allocation and no investor pre-sale.

    The founding fleet includes machines like `pawnshop_g4_115` and `dual-g4-125` — PowerPC Macs sourced from secondhand shops. The 6% premine is split evenly across four founder wallets, all with a one-year on-chain unlock delay.

    This is what crypto looked like before the money showed up.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is BCOS?">
    **BCOS** stands for **Blockchain-Certified Open Source** — a verification system that issues on-chain certificates to open-source projects.

    RustChain's BCOS system provides a tamper-evident, blockchain-anchored record that a given repository and release are genuine open-source. Think of it as a cryptographic quality seal for open-source software.

    Browse certified projects at [rustchain.org/bcos](https://rustchain.org/bcos/). As of the latest count, 44 certificates have been issued.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
